CDC Dog Import Rules Explained (2026 Update)

Last updated: 2026-03-04

Since August 2024, the CDC has enforced a standardized entry system for all dogs. Core Rules: Dogs must appear healthy, be at least 6 months of age, and be microchipped with an ISO-compliant chip implanted BEFORE their last rabies shot. All travelers must present the 'CDC Dog Import Form' receipt to the airline before boarding and to Customs upon arrival.

Essential Preparation

  • ISO 11784/11785 compliant microchip
  • Rabies vaccination (at least 30 days before travel)
  • International health certificate (endorsed by local authorities)
  • IATA approved travel crate
