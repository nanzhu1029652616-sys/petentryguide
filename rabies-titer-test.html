Rabies Titer Test: When You Need It

Last updated: 2026-03-04

A Rabies Titer Test (FAVN) measures the level of antibodies in a pet's blood. When it matters: While the US typically doesn't require a titer test for pets from low-risk countries, it is often required if you are transiting through rabies-free countries (like Hawaii or certain islands) or if you are following specific CDC high-risk protocols to shorten quarantine.

  • Passing level is 0.5 IU/mL or higher.
  • Samples must be processed by a CDC-approved laboratory.
  • The test is valid for the duration of the rabies vaccine's potency.

Essential Preparation

  • ISO 11784/11785 compliant microchip
  • Rabies vaccination (at least 30 days before travel)
  • International health certificate (endorsed by local authorities)
  • IATA approved travel crate
